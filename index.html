Low Density Supersonic Decelerator

What does it do?

  • Designed to enter an atmosphere at supersonic speeds (tested on Earth, designed for Mars).
  • Can carry a much heavier payload than more traditional methods (2â€“3 tons vs. 1 ton).

How does it work?

  • Has a giant balloon to increase drag, which it deploys when it enters the atmosphere.
  • Deploys a giant parachute once it's slow enough.

What is the purpose of the technology?

  • To allow landing on planets like Mars at higher speeds and with a heavier payload.
  • This would allow the spacecraft not to need fuel to slow down, and would allow more weight to be allocated to cargo like food, water, and equipment instead of to fuel.

How did the first test go?

  • The video on the left shows footage from the cameras aboard the spacecraft, as well as an interview with someone from NASA.
  • When the parachute was deployed, it was destroyed. The spacecraft was most likely going too fast.
  • Despite the failure of the parachute, NASA says the test went well.
  • The pictures below show the spacecraft after it crashed in the Pacific Ocean.

How has it contributed to our understanding of space?

  • By allowing us to bring more supplies into space, it can help future research projects bring more equipment and bring back more samples.
  • It has given insights into how parachutes behave at speeds that are faster than the speed of sound.

Anything else?
